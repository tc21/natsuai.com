Let's look at the discard pile A. After discarding v4, a z riichi. Something feels unnatural about this. They don't look like they're going for chanta or sanshoku, so what makes z a more useful tile than v?

The answer is chiitoitsu. They are waiting on something better than the suji z. In other words, honor tiles, or safe 1/9 tiles that everyone is likely to discard. However, rare cases like discarding z from a shape like zzzer also exist, so don't be so sure that they have chiitoitsu.

What about B? They broke up a pair, but kept the second tile until they got into tenpai. Their first discard was z, so they likely don't have tiles related to z. What's likely is bm. or bmm, making , and m dangerous. In particular, if you look at their discards they have a lot of smaller numbered tiles, making 789 sanshoku a likely possibility. Even though , is suji, don't discard it so easily.

What about C? They discarded v very early, but their riichi declaration tile is b. Rather than having vvbb, there's a possibility that they didn't have b when they discarded v, but drew it later and was able to keep it. In other words there's a chance they have bm., and are waiting on ,.

Up until now, I've been talking about reading your opponent's wait using two tiles from the same suit. Once upon a time this kind of theory was not popular. In the past, camouflaging, or techniques to conceal your wait, was highly valued. For example, in example 5.6 A from the previous section, someone might discard v first, and then x, to conceal the fact that they are waiting on cn. Also, more people waited for an improvement before declaring riichi back then, meaning example B (from this section) could well be improving a kanchan n wait to a n. ryanmen wait.

Today, people better understand how to make a hand, and better appreciate the value of a first riichi. So you could say these theories here are made possible my modern mahjong theory. That said, knowing the theories of modern mahjong, and still discarding in the order of vx from xvvb could potentially be an effective way to trick your opponents. It might be an interesting thing to try every once in a while. Additionally, some players still play like that all the time, so be careful not to be too confident in your hand reading.
