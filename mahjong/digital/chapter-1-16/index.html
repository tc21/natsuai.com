Let's think about how to choose which block to break up. A strong block is one that is easily completed (e.g. ryanmen[1] blocks) or one that you really want to complete (e.g. double 1). A weak block would be one that is difficult to complete (e.g. penchan[2] blocks).

(Translator's note: perhaps stop and think about what you would discard with each of these hands before continuing.)

  1. qqyyucvnm,gh77
  2. erxcvn,fhk1133
  3. wecvggghkll477

A's weakest blocks are q and 7. There is very little difference in how easily they are completed. q is the correct discard here, since yakuhai is more valuable.

B (assume east round dealer) is a much harder example. It's full of weak blocks. First of all, discarding k is not an option: it leaves you with 6 blocks, of which n, and fh are both very weak. Next, if you discard 3 and then pon 1, you are left with no pairs. Here you should think of fhk as a strong block and discard ,.

Now let's look at C. Focus on the shape of the souzu. Depending on how many blocks you view it as, you choice of discard may change. You can choose to view the souzu as these 3 blocks: ggg, hk, ll. But you already have 3 more blocks in your hand, so you don't want these kind of weak blocks. Therefore you should think of kll as one block. Your useless tiles here are h and 4, and you should discard the dangerous tile h and keep the safer tile 4.

If I showed you hand C and asked you, “What should you discard here?”, you might want to discard 4, but then think that too obvious an answer, and decide to answer h. It is important that you learn to discard h not just in this context, but also when actually playing mahjong. You should always be deepening your understanding of the shapes that appear in mahjong, and over time, learn to not discard 4 when faced with a hand like example C.

Keeping 5-block theory in mind while playing mahjong is a great way to improve your tile efficiency and win rate. That said, although usually you should be discarding your weakest block when you have 6 blocks, there may be times where you can't find your weakest block. In that case one of your choices would be to secure a two-sided shape and play with 6 blocks for the time being. The best thing to do is to gain as much knowledge and experience as you can, so that you can find a way to flexibly react to any situation you may face.

  1. [^] Literally, “two sided”. Two adjacent tiles, completable on both sides. e.g. er.
  2. [^] Literally, “edge tiles”. A terminal tile and the tile next to it. e.g. qw
