This hand is a xb and af 1-shanten, after calling 6 and the dora 2[1]. It was easy to get to this point, but we've been guarded against ever since. The other players are playing defensively, and kamicha has been exclusively playing safe tiles, trying to prevent us from calling anything. In this situation, we drew d. Discarding 3 gives us better tile efficiency. What should we do?

If we discard 3 here, we are likely to discard d when we get to tenpai. But discarding g early, and then a d tedashi signals that we are waiting next to that tile, and we won't be able to ron af. On the other hand, if we tsumogiri d, and then tedashi 3, people are likely to read that we don't have an incomplete shape near d. They are unlikely to expect a af wait. By the way, if we draw af first and wait on xb, then our sakigiri of d would have no effect.

Another thing to pay attention to is toimen discarding a just now. If we tsumogiri here, there's a possibility that kamicha might play a, giving us opportunity to chii. If we tedashi a tile, then perhaps a won't come out anymore.

Of course, if we discard d, we won't be able to use o and d anymore. Missing out on a pon or triplet draw into tenpai is not a small loss.

Even so, I recommend tsumogiri d. Although we don't want to miss out on drawing o or d, the better possibility of calling chii or ron on zv is a much larger benefit.

If you're playing with three other players that don't pay any attention to tedashi and tsumogiri, of course it would be better to discard 3. However, the stronger your opponents are, the more effective this tsumogiri becomes. This technique is effective precisely because it hurts your tile efficiency.
