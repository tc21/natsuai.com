Up until now I've talked about how to think with 5 blocks. Thinking with 5 blocks means with a hand like A, which 6 useful shapes, to cut qw, the weakest one. You are somewhat deciding on the path forward for your hand, and taking the shortest path towards the hand you envision. I call this a “decision play”.

What about B? You can be decisive and discard qw or io, but unless there is a big difference in the board situation, locking into a particular penchan shape early on feels a little insecure. Why not put off making a choice for now, and discard s to lock into the ryanmen shape. Alternatively, if there are few , remaining, m is also a good discard.

Like this, instead of deciding on the path you'll take, postponing the decision until later while keeping your shanten the same, is what I call a “deferral play”. You should keep this in your repertoire of tactics. While it might look like a half-baked approach, it's often useful in the early stages of a hand.

C is a shape that appears often. Discarding 7 to allow for drawing c or l gets you to tenpai the fastest, but if you take into account the risk of 7 getting called, or drawing the dora yourself, discarding c and locking into the ryanmen shape for now looks better.

D is like C, but the shapes of souzu isn't great. If you say “I'll go for it if I draw into pinfu” and discard f, or “I'll attack with a fl shanpon wait” and discard s, that would be a “deferral play”. If you go all out and discard 7, or if you say “I'll never discard the dora” and discard l, that would be a “decision play”[1].

My recommendation is to discard s or f. The only times to discard dora from an insecure shape like this, is when it is very early on in a hand, or fighting for placement in all last. That said deciding to not discard dora and break up a 1-shanten hand by discarding l is too negative of an approach. In usual circumstances, just think about what shapes are work pushing the dora, and keep 1-shanten by cutting one of s or f.

  1. [^] To translate both 保留 and 決断 into words that start with the letter D, that would be a “wordplay”.
