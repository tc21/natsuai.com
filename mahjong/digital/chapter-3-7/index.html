There are many tiles you can call with the hand above. Let's look at these six: cafjk1. Try ranking these six tiles by how much you want to call them, and then think about which tiles you would call, and which tiles you wouldn't call.

Have you come up with your answers? I'd like to share my own opinions.

First of all, the tile you want to call the most is the double-wind 1. You should all agree with this. The next best tile to call, in my opinion, is c, though I think lots of people would also choose a. Compared to c, you definitely have a yaku, making it easier to call the rest of your hand. But if your first call is a, is c a tile that's likely to come out? Suddenly calling a ryanmen shape like that makes people question whether you have a confirmed yaku at all. Even if you later call 1 or j, people will still be wary of sanshoku, making the 2 and 3 of manzu and pinzu difficult to come out -- especially against experienced players.

On the other hand, calling c might just be to take care of the weak penchan c shape, and is less likely to be read as sanshoku compared to calling a. Thus 1-3 tiles won't become extremely difficult to come out, and calling a not-really-useful tile like a is still very probable. Additionally, this hand has more than one out. As long as you don't keep on drawing undesirable tiles, you will have a yaku. In other words, you have a “3/4 chance of winning”. Even if you draw f, killing both chanta and sanshoku, you can just win with 1. Also after calling c you should take j or k for tenpai. To summarize, our order of tiles so far is 1ca. These are the tiles you want to call.

As for the other tiles, like f which gets rid of chanta and sanshoku, leaving you only with 1, or j and k which requires you to finish the sanshoku, are better left uncalled. In conclusion, the tiles in order from best to worst are 1cajkf, and we want to call tiles that are better than a. To get better at mahjong, calling because “it advances my hand” is not enough. You need to not just secure a yaku, but also think about how likely tiles are to come out later on, the size of your hand, as well as what turn it is.
