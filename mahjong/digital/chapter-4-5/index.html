You were waiting for a better wait, or for sanshoku, but you drew into tsumo. Tsumo-dora 1. Should you take this 2000-point hand, or should you discard a for a furiten riichi?

You might think, giving up an early 2000-point win for an 8000-point hand is worth it. But is this hand really worth 8000 points?

If you took the tsumo, you would win 2000 points, and have 27000 points total. Let's base our thinking from this. Passing up the win here means giving back the dealer the 1000 points you would have won, and 500 points each to the other two players. Counting the riichi stick, you would effectively be placing a riichi bet of 3000 points.

If you win a mangan, you will have 33000 points. Compared to the basis of 27000 points, you will have gained 6000 points. In other words, declaring this furiten riichi would be betting 3000 points in riichi payment for a 6000-point hand!

Although you have a ryanmen wait, you cannot ron. Drawing the higher-value tsumo finally nets you 6000 points. Betting 3000 points just for something like this is clearly inefficient. You should take what you have here.
