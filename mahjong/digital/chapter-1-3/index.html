East 1, Turn 1, Dealer

A is the dealer's starting hand. As dealer, you wouldn't discard the double 1, so you're choosing between q and tiles(1p). There correct discard here is z. Discarding q causes you to miss out on qwe draws. On the other hand, because you have v, discarding z still allows you to make use of x and c, limiting your tile efficiency loss to a minimum.

B is just like A: there correct discard is z. There is not much reason to keep the z here. Even if you make zxvv, it's difficult to make two sets out of that shape.

What about C? The difference between C and the previous two hands is that here v already forms a completed set. In this situation q and z are basically the same in efficiency. Then, you should consider that, if you end up tenpai on a tile next to q or z, which one would be better? Because you already see 3 of the v, other people will have a harder time making use of x or c. Therefore, if you intend to be riichi-ing with this hand, you should discard q, aiming to potentially win on others' x or c discard. Some of you may be worried of holding on to too many tiles from the zv suji, but there's no reason to worry about that if it's still the first turn.

Now let's look at D. Once upon a time it was thought that drawing c would create a zcb shape, and so a would be the better discard. But from a simple tile efficiency standpoint, discarding z still allows you to utilize c, so you're only losing zx. Discarding a loses you asd. Some people like to emphasize the benefits of the ryankan zcb shape if you draw c, but you won't lose the v draw even if you discard z: discarding z only loses you the x draw. Compared to the small benefit if the zcb shape, the loss of tile efficiency by discarding a is much larger. Discarding z is more efficient here.

Ryankan shapes are important when you want to make pinfu, but a hand like D cannot become pinfu to begin with, and indeed most hands are better suited for this playstyle.
