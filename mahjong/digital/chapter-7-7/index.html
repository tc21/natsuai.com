Once you get used to the point-difference chart you should mostly be able to calculate your comebacks, but things can get complicated with honba and riichi sticks.

First is deposited riichi sticks. Assuming you win this hand, then you will receive them. All you have to do is subtract the riichi sticks from your point difference. For example, a 3800 difference with two riichi sticks out is equivalent to 1800 points, so you only need to think about making up 1800 points.

Then you have honba sticks. If you're winning off an unrelated person, subtract 300 points per honba. If you tsumo, a honba stick is equivalent to 100-points-all, so subtract 400 points. For a direct hit, subtract 600 points.

Look at the point-difference chart in the previous section and try calculating for yourself. For each of the following situations, calculate what you need for a comeback by winning (1) from anyone, (2) by tsumo, and (3) with a direct hit. If you are able to do this quickly at the beginning of all-last, you will be able to build your hand without making any mistakes, and find the shortest path to a comeback.

Find the value of the hand required to come back.
