Our hand is 1-shanten, but n and h are both quite dangerous. We are still early in this round, and we don't have many safe tiles, so we should aim to win this hand. Which one out of n and h should we discard?

Sometimes, I'll hear the following reasoning: When choosing between two equivalent shapes or tiles, people typically choose with dora in mind, therefore the dora-suji h is more dangerous than an average tile. However, looking at this riichi, there's no evidence of being able to choose one shape over another. Thus there is basically no difference in how dangerous n and h are.

Thus, the only difference between n and h is the potential loss when the opponent tsumos after you don't deal in. If you stop the dora-suji h from dealing in, but they then tsumo on the dora d, the two extra han from dora and tsumo would make your losses as dealer larger. Take a look at this chart.

For dealers, when someone else riichis a hand with a two-sided wait including dora, this chart compares the amount of points lost between the dealer dealing in, and the amount of points lost when the player declaring riichi tsumos. Example 1 is riichi-tanyao, example 2 is riichi-pinfu. When the opponent is waiting on dh, dealing in with h often carries both a lower point loss and a lower point difference created.

Returning to the question given at the beginning, h is a tile you want to discard, while n is a tile you want to keep until you cannot keep it anymore.

And of course, the dora itself is a tile better not discarded. Due to people more likely to have a tanki or shanpon wait on dora, it is not only more likely to deal in, but bigger when you deal in. Another type of dangerous tile is dora-matagi[1]. Dealing in with dora-matagi means your opponent likely has dora in their hand, and you will lose more points than when dealing in with other tiles.

  1. [^] The matagi-suji (“跨ぎ筋”, “across-suji”) of a tile refers to the one or two sets of suji (i.e. ryanmen waits) that one may have when they already have that tile in hand. See also mahjonggem.com/blog/2019/6/12/matagi-suji. e.g. The matagi-suji of d is af and sg.
