The potential discards from hand A are z and .. Which tile should you discard? Discarding . loses you m,., but discarding z does not lose xc. Thus many people would want to discard z first. But look closely at this hand. Drawing next to . and forming another inefficient shape isn't really what we want with this hand. What we want would be an efficient (two-sided) shape, or a yakuhai pair. Thus, we should think about drawing c, and then extending the pinzu shape even more. We should discard ..

Hand B also has enough sets of tiles that can be used to complete a hand [2] . o and l look completely useless in this situation. The only difference is if you keep o and then draw t or i, forming the shape wryuio (draw e or t), or the shape wrtyuo (draw e or i). Both these potential ryankan shapes disappear if you throw o.

Hand C already accepts dhl draws, so s looks useless on first glance. But drawing s allows you to wait on dhl, and drawing d allows you to wait on sgk. Even if you draw gk first you can at least wait on d. As you can see, s is actually quite useful, so the correct discard is v.

This kind of shape, where you can form a nobetan by drawing the right tile, is called “phantom nobetan”. Many people miss it and throw it away, so please be aware. Usually in a sfg shape, the s is useless. But if you don't have a pair (specifically, the head a.k.a. jyantou) yet, often times you may want to keep it.

Also, in general, when “drawing A allows you to wait on B” (such as the “drawing d allows you to wait on sgk” example above) is true, “drawing B allows you to wait on A” is also true. Many people get fixated on drawing A, but forget that drawing B also allows you to get to tenpai.

When thinking about tenpai chances and your potential waits, never forget that drawing your potential waits first can also get you into tenpai.
