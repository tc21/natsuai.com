They broke up a yakuhai pair! Why would they do that?

For certain, their hand is a tanyao pinfu shape[1].

First, you can read that they kept another pair over the yakuhai pair because it's easier to make a yaku that way[2]. Then, their wait should be better than a yakuhai shanpon[3]. Therefore, their hand likely looked like B before they discarded 5.

With a hand like C, where pinfu isn't confirmed, most people would discard k for the shanpon riichi. And most people who discarded 5 from hand D would damaten if they drew w after that. It's hard to think that someone who broke up a yakuhai pair would end up declaring riichi with an expensive hand and a bad wait.

After reading the hand as tanyao pinfu, it becomes easy to play. First, all suji are safe. Then, 1 and 9, which cannot deal into tanyao pinfu, are likely to be safe. Sometimes it turns out they didn't have tanyao, but had a dora pair, but those cases are rare. If you deal into that, you'll just have to accept it.

By the way, if they broke up a yakuhai pair, but didn't riichi, most likely they have a tanyao pinfu 1-shanten such as E. Be prepared for a ryanmen riichi to come at any moment.
