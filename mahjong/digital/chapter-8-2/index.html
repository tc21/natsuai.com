In a kuttsuki[1] 1-shanten, sometimes some of your draws might create a furiten. Rather than avoiding furiten at all costs, try to choose between tiles while considering all of your possibilities.

  1. [^] “Kuttsuki” (くっつき, lit. to adhere to) refers to having multiple unrelated tiles, and hoping to advance your hand by drawing next to those tiles, and waiting on the tile/tiles you didn't draw. For example, if you kept j in the hand above, you would be tenpai if you drew any tile next to j.
