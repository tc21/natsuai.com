What should be discarded from hand A? This is a hand from the South wind player, East 1, turn 5. With a dora tripled but no yaku, we should try to declare riichi as quickly as possible. Some of you might think making a ryanmen set is the most efficient, such as discarding v to make hand B. The tenpai draws are wti and xb. Since drawing gk doesn't get you into tenpai, it turns out there wasn't really a reason to keep h. Therefore, we should think about possibly getting into tenpai by drawing v or j into a triplet. (See also 1.6 Perfect 1-Shanten.) The possible discards here are c and h. Look at the table, and compare xb and gk, and discard from the suit that clearly has less tiles available.

But what about when you can't tell which suit is better, such as in the first few turns? Can we make a decision one way or another? Let's change our perspectives and think a little bit about the future. If you discard c, and draw wti or gk, you will discard j and declare riichi. Your discard pile will be something like C. If you discard h, the same draws will give you a discard pile like D.

Think about the differences between these two discard piles from the perspective of your opponents. With C, expect for genbutsu, no tiles are really safe. Since j is the riichi declaration tile, gk and hl are quite dangerous, but you can't be sure since so many tiles are still dangerous. Whether your wait is gk or wti, if you force your genbutsu-less opponents to fight against you, you should be confident in winning.

What about D? Obviously xb and cn are dangerous, but still you can't really tell. That said, with v and h being genbutsu, the zvm and dhl suji become fairly safe. With 4 additional tiles to discard, it becomes much easier for your opponents to defend. With such a good hand, don't make your hand easier to defend against.

To summarize, with hand A, you should discard c, to maximize your speed to tenpai, and minimize the information you give away in your discard pile. From the perspective of your opponents, try to make your discard pile as difficult to work with as possible.
