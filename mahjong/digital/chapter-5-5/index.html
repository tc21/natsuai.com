I want to extend a little on the previous section about urasuji and matagi-suji. The theory that “matagi-suji to the riichi declaration tile is dangerous, but the urasuji is not as dangerous” is pretty effective. For example, if you had to choose between discarding z and m to a b-riichi, there is a big difference in safety, and you should discard the z.

This theory has become popular lately, and it seems to have spread in the form of “urasuji of riichi declaration tiles are safe”. Many famous pros and good mahjong players have been using this theory, treating it as common sense.

Certainly, cases like xcb and bm, are rare. But does that really mean we can call them “safe”?

First of all, a common shape is one like A or B, with a potential iipeikou. As long as you don't see lots of x or c, you cannot rule out this possibility. C is a simple 3-sided wait. D is choosing a nobetan wait. These shapes also exist. E is a ryanmen-shanpon combination shape. F is a shape where b was kept to try and make bnm. Lastly, G is a shape where b was kept for the possibility of sanshoku.

Although each of the previous situations can be considered uncommon, to lump all of them together and say “dealing in with urasuji is rare” is wild. Although a simple matagi-suji cn or vm wait might be more common, you cannot ignore the possibility of an urasuji wait. For example, if you compare the safety of zv vs. af to a b riichi, we can't rule out the possibility of them having a reason to keep b until their riichi. If you ask me which one is safer, I cannot judge. I can only say that they are both sets of suji that haven't passed yet.
