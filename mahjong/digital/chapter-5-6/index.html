Reading your opponent's hand isn't an easy task. One of the most important tiles is the one they play to get into tenpai, such as their riichi declaration tile. This is usually a tile that's useful to their hand up until the moment they declared riichi. Often times, the tile is related to their waits, or provide important information about the direction of their hand. In this section, we'll talk about a case that provides very useful information: when two tiles of the same suit are discarded separately.

  1. q4soxk4V
  2. q4sovk4X
  3. q4sock4V

First, let's look at the discard pile A. After discarding x, they first discarded the safer 4, and then declared riichi with v. What this means is that they didn't need x, but v was useful until the very end. First, we can consider the following shapes: vvb, vbb, and vnn. In these cases the most likely wait is cn, followed by a shanpon of n and something else. If they had vbb, that means they are using b as their head, and their wait is somewhere else[1]. Additionally, if they had vnm they would probably discard v before 4, so the likelihood of a b, wait is much lower than a cn wait.

B is just A with the discard order reversed, but it gives us vastly different information. In A, the most likely wait was cn. In B, that would mean discarding v first from xvvb and throwing away the possibility of forming a v triplet, a very unnatural move. In other words, a cn ryanmen wait is unlikely. Then, if we think about shapes where v is useless, but x is be useful, it would be zzx. In other words, this hand is likely to be a shanpon wait between z and something else. Although it's suji, we should not easily discard z.

C is a shape where someone broke apart a ryanmen. But still they were able to keep v in their hand until the very end. If they had vnm, they would have probably discarded v before 4, so only one shape is thinkable: cvv. In other words, three turns ago they had ccvv, and not wanting to draw xb twice, they discarded one each of c and v. Thus xb is quite dangerous.

Pay attention when someone discards two (connected) tiles of the same suit, as it is a hint to the shape of their hand.

  1. [^] Translator's note: Why can't they be waiting shanpon on the b? Generally speaking, if they had another pair elsewhere, they would have discarded b waiting on the cn ryanmen, instead of shanpon.
