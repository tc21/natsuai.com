Kindai Mahjong Premier League, Table A, Stage 3

This is a Kindai Mahjong Premier League game[1]. I have a honitsu 1-shanten hand that could be as high as a baiman. I wanted to discard the completely useless y, but pay attention to Suzuki Tatsuya (North)'s calls and discards. The hand looks like a manzu honitsu. o was discarded on the 6th turn. He then called u, discarding 2. If he was going for honitsu, why did he discard o from a uuo shape? It would be natural to keep the i draw. So, discarding o likely means that even after discarding o, he can still make use of i. This means he likely had yyuu. Since we are reading that he has a y pair, we cannot discard y, even though e and o are safe. That said, neither can we discard 5 or the dora j. We pretty much have to bail, and can't do anything other than discard a to tentatively stay in chiitoitsu 1-shanten. We will have to fully bail when we draw another dangerous tile.

By the way, this was Suzuki Tatsuya's hand:

yynnn77Uuu$44

Although it was toitoi, instead of my read of honitsu, y was as expected a deal-in tile. I successfully avoided a 5200-point deal-in. The fact that y was the deal-in tile was just a coincidence. What's important is to use the information in discards to understand that y is dangerous, and is a tile that cannot be played. Small theories like this one accumulate into real improvements in your results.

However, as I said before, reads are not always right. If I drew j or 5 after discarding a, I would have a very valuable hand. Considering the risk and reward, I would probably fight and play y.
