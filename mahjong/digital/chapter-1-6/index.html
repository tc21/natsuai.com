Following up on the previous section, sometimes you will have a ryanmen-kanchan shape, and intentionally not keep it.

Hand A: choose between z and g to discard. Like previous sections hand D, discarding g guarantees pinfu, but is that really the right thing to do here? Drawing y or g first gives you tanyao. Since tanyao has a higher value than pinfu, as well as allowing you to open your hand, discarding z is better here. Don't discard g just because “It's ryanmen-kanchan!”

Discarding n from hand B guarantees pinfu. Regardless of what you draw you can riichi and have a 2000-point hand. What if you discarded z? You will lose the 2000-point hand from the x draw, and gain the 1300-point hand from the n and a draw. If it was only that, pinfu would be better. But also, drawing v would guarantee iipeikou, making your hand worth 3900 points. Although there are only three more v, the 1900-point increase in value is quite significant. If you're planning to riichi your hand, discarding z is better.

However, if you were not planning to riichi, for example, if you're in first place and want to progress the round with a damaten hand, then it is better to guarantee pinfu and discard n. Often times hands should be handled differently in different situations.

Now let's look at hand C. This hand is 1-shanten for tanyao-pinfu-sanshoku. You want vm and fj, and you're holding on to 4 as a safe tile. Some people wouldn't think twice about discarding a, but of course the right discard is 4. It would be more obvious if you put the souzu tiles together: adfggh. Keeping a gives you the s draw. Many people end up only thinking about tanyao-pinfu-sanshoku, and arrange their hands the same way as example C, causing them to miss this shape. Be careful not to do this.

That said, the term “ryanmen-kanchan” is pretty difficult to understand. Can someone figure out a better name for it...?
