It's turn 6, and you have the above tanyao-nomi 1-shanten hand. What discards from kamicha would you call?

I think most people would call c. Calling away the kanchan c makes your hand strong. What about fj? In the last section, we talked about how keeping a bad shape (like kanchan) in your hand means you don't get close to winning, so you shouldn't start calling from good shapes. But what about now?

The answer is to call. Although you will still have a bad kanchan c tenpai, when you are tenpai, you can call “ron”. You can only call chii from kamicha, but you can call ron from anyone. The best way to take care of the bad shape is to win off it, so we should get to tenpai as quickly as we can.

In addition, you might think that you've built your hand to this point, calling and making it worth only 1000 points isn't worth it. But you only have 3 tiles you can draw to get into tenpai. Calculating the probabilities, you have an about 40% chance to get to tenpai by turn 10, and 60% by turn 13. It isn't that easy to draw, even if you have 3 choices. Even if your hand does advance closed, you are more likely to draw fj first and wait on the kanchan c. Rather than being able to declare a fj riichi when you draw c, it's better to actually win a 300/500 hand[1].
