This is one of my hands from a real game. I had 2 dora in my starting hand. On turn 3, I drew 7p, and chiitoitsu is now the obvious route. What should we discard here?

10 years ago, I probably would have discarded r or h. Discarding dangerous middle tiles first, holding on to terminals and honors.

That said, I discarded the round wind 2. Then on the next turn, I didn't discard the n I drew, and instead discarded .. This is to keep 5 and 7, my likely waits, while making a normal discard pile, so that other people can't read that I have a chiitoitsu hand. The trick is to make pairs with middle tiles as much as you can, and make terminals and honors appear in your discard pile.

I drew r on turn 5 just as planned, and later drew 7, waiting on the 1-discarded 5. My discard pile was ik2.nhc. Then peicha[1] entered tenpai, discarded 5, and dealt in to my hand for 9600 points.

Lets think instead about if my discard pile was ikrnh, and then discarded 2 as I entered tenpai. People would have been paying attention to my hand as atypical, and discarding shonpai 2 would have felt like tenpai. People would be able to read that I have a chiitoitsu hand, and even a 1-cut 5 would have become hard to come out. The 3rd-turn 2 discard is to prevent such an outcome.

That said, you need to be very careful playing like this. If you're not careful, you'll end up with a hand full of dangerous middle tiles. You have to carefully balance sakigiri, awaseuchi [2] , and keeping safe tiles, a very difficult thing to do.

  1. [^] The player in north seat.
  2. [^] For the flow of the paragraph, I left the terms untranslated here. Sakigiri (“discarding-first”) refers to discarding dangerous tiles early before they become dangerous. Chapter 1.1 is an example of sakigiri. Awaseuchi (“discard-matching”) refers to discarding a tile that someone else just discarded, as it is confirmed safe for the time being.
