Hand A is a 4th turn hand by a non-dealer player. Should we aim for pinfu and discard 7? Should we try to use 7 while accepting a ryanmen[1] riichi and discard l? Or should we aim to utilize 7 no matter what and discard a ryanmen shape? Think about it for a moment.

First let's consider discarding 7. If the hand advances normally and becomes pinfu-dora 1, you will probably declare riichi, and have a hand that's 3900 points on ron, 5200 points on tsumo. If you discard l, most likely you will have riichi-dora 1. That's 2600 points on ron, 4000 on tsumo. Comparing these numbers, discarding 7 would be better. But if you look closely, although your hand increases by 1 han, the value of the hand didn't increase by a lot. Usually increasing a hand by 1 han should double its value, but pinfu lowers the fu of your hand and makes the increase not as significant. If you were going to declare riichi anyway, there is no reason to be fixated on something like pinfu. By the way, if you end up gaining an uradora, the difference pinfu brings becomes even smaller.

The question is, how your hand can change if you do not immediately draw into tenpai. First, compare (between the two choices of discard) the difference when you draw your pair into a triplet.

Since both shapes allow you to become tenpai by drawing one of your ryanmen shapes into a pair (that is, drawing yuxc), having the 7 triplet is obviously better.

Now lets compare drawing a pair in your ryanmen-shape first.

  2. qweyuuxcfgh77

Comparing the two, E is better as it allows you to call 7 at anytime to get into tenpai.

Finally let's look at discarding a ryanmen shape from the initial hand. Fixating on the one han from 7 to the point of hurting your chances of a ryanmen riichi is not something that can be recommended. It's best to treat 7 as your pair until you draw into a shape like C or E.

To summarize, the solution to hand A is to consider both the ryanmen riichi and the potential ways to utilize 7, and discard the pair of l.
