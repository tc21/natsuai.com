Techniques to make it difficult for others to guess your wait have long been studied in mahjong. An often used technique is camouflaging your wait by throwing a lot of tiles of the same suit. For example, throwing vc early on, and then waiting on xb. Another one is the suji trap, where, after throwing b, you wait on the suji x. Also, there are techniques where you make people think you have a honiitsu but actually are waiting on another suit. Here, I'll introduce a way to wait on tiles that your opponent might not expect when you open your hand.

The first thing that comes to mind is drawing n and discarding c for a vm wait. Also drawing c and discarding b for a cnk wait, or drawing b and discarding c for a xbk wait. Additionally, calling or drawing , allows you to choose between a xb or cn wait.

And then, what you cannot miss is calling pon on c or b. If you call b and discard c, your wait becomes xb. Although the number of tiles you wait on don't increase, consider that to your opponents, your hand looks like B, and your discard pile looks like C.
