“Ahh! I failed!”

I hear this constantly in mahjong. Let's talk a bit about this. Mahjong is a game where you draw from randomly shuffled tiles, and thus it is a constant stream of random events, one after another. If every time something happens, you lose your composure, then you will be unable to make the correct decisions. It's important to have a strong mentality that isn't affected by anything that happens. You often hear about having a “strong mentality”, but there are few games in which a strong mentality is as important as mahjong.

“Ahh, I failed!” “How am I so bad?” “This wasn't the plan!”

There is no need to grief about anything. You've already decided e was useless when you discarded qw, so just discard e. It's not an unexpected development, nor is it a failure. Discarding e was exactly your plan.

With 2 dora, you want to quickly find a yaku, or make a set. You can't choose anything other than 5 and 7, but without any information, let's just discard 5. And the next turn you draw 5!

“Ahh! I failed!” “My intuition isn't working! Nothing's going to work out today!”

Don't think like that. Of course the discard was one according to plan, but is this 5 really such a bad draw? There are 34 different mahjong tiles. In this shape, there about 10 that are useful, and more than 20 that are useless. Certainly 5 is part of those useless tiles, but it's the safest among them, and gives away the least amount of information. You could probably call it “the best useless tile”. Think of it as being much better than drawing and discarding some new tile, like i or z.
